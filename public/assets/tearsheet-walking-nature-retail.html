Target Profile

Nature-lifestyle JV partner. Designer vision: walking trails and open areas oriented toward nature and overall fitness concepts. Residential density on interior lots creates a built-in consumer base that feeds foot traffic directly to the retail strip along frontage and corner positions. The connected trail system becomes a marketing differentiator and community amenity — positioning ITPH as a lifestyle destination, not just a commercial park.

Key Features — Walking Nature Retail Concept

  • 20% open space with connected trail system: ~19.9 acres dedicated to walking trails, pocket parks, fitness stations, and a central nature corridor linking residential neighborhoods to the retail strip. Soccer park retained at NE corner. This is Mark's nature/fitness vision realized — the trail network becomes a signature community amenity and marketing centerpiece.
  • Residential feeding foot traffic to retail: Nine interior lots (47.9 acres) converted to multifamily/townhome residential at $7/SF. These residents become the built-in consumer base for the 21 retail lots along the frontage — a self-reinforcing economic loop where rooftops drive retail sales and retail amenities attract residents.
  • Retail strip anchored on frontage and corners: 21 retail lots command $15–$18/SF with corner premium sites at Lots 1, 12, 14, 15, and 23. The largest corner parcels (Lots 14 and 15 at 13.2 and 11.2 acres) generate $19.1M alone — nearly 37% of total revenue from two anchor positions.
  • 80% buildable, 20% amenity — no wasted land: Open space acreage is not idle — it's working land that increases adjacent lot values, attracts higher-quality tenants, and positions the development for nature-conscious JV capital. Parking integrated into retail pads keeps the trail corridors pedestrian-only.
