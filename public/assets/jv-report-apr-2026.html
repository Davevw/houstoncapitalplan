5. Scenario Analysis — Stay In vs. Buyout

Scenario A — Stay In

Structure: JV continues; Quinlan retains equity through development and lot sales
Revenue Potential: $39.4M total inventory; $25.8M already in active pipeline
Profit: Higher total but longer duration — lot sales and MUD reimbursements over 18+ months
MUD Bonds: Full $23.4M reimbursement flows to JV upon issuance
Risk: Continued operational obligations — engineering, compliance, infrastructure funding
Developer Value: Full realization of advisory, lot premiums, and capital markets execution

Scenario B — Buyout

Structure: Debt financing covers buyout of existing partner and funds infrastructure going forward
Capital Sources (Active): Walker Dunlop (debt) and Index Fund — both currently in discussions
Debt Target: Up to $36M intended to cover buyout and infrastructure costs. Amount to be finalized based on construction budget and schedule
Advantage: Clean exit with certain proceeds vs. shared upside with execution risk
Target Timeline: Buyout closing targeted for Q3 2026, aligning with construction start (July 2026) to enable simultaneous infrastructure mobilization
Developer Value: Development continues under new capitalization — value from developer’s efforts, relationships, and capital markets execution carries forward
