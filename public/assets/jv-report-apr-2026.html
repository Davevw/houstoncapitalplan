5. Scenario Analysis — Stay In vs. Buyout

Scenario A — Stay In

Structure: JV continues; Quinlan retains equity through development and lot sales
Revenue Potential: $39.4M total lot inventory at current pricing; $25.8M already in active pipeline
Profit: Higher total but longer duration (18+ months to realization)
MUD Bonds: Full $23.4M reimbursement flows to JV
Risk: Ongoing funding of LFG compliance, engineering, infrastructure
Developer Value: Full realization of advisory, lot premiums, capital markets execution

Scenario B — Buyout

Structure: $5M Phase 1 (debt + pref equity) + $5M Phase 2 (proceeds)
Proposal A: $36M — buyout tranche, Phase 1 infrastructure, vertical for 45 homes + 80 lots
Proposal B: ARESC Holdings (overseas) — pending
Timeline: Buyout at first funding eliminates future liabilities
Advantage: Clean exit with certain proceeds vs. shared upside with execution risk
Developer Value: Continuation of execution — value from developer’s efforts and capital relationships
