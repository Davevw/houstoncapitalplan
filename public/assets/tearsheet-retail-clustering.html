Target Profile

Optimized for broker absorption. Retail specialist-driven reconfiguration clusters retail at high-traffic intersections where ITPH internal roads meet Bissonnet, S Kirkwood, and Cook Rd. Corner retail priced at $18/SF premium, flex interior at $10/SF. Target: faster retail lease-up with realistic market positioning driven by broker feedback on lot desirability.

Key Features — Retail Intersection Clustering

  • Broker-driven reconfiguration: Retail concentrated at the intersections where market demand is strongest — Bissonnet, Cook, S Kirkwood, and Collector road corners. Interior corridor lots (S Kirkwood, Cook) shift from retail to flex/light industrial, directly addressing broker feedback that these locations are less enthusiastically embraced by the retail market.
  • Corner pads sized for QSR anchors: Deep corner lots at Lots 1, 12, and 23 designed for national QSR tenants (McDonald's, Chick-fil-A) requiring larger footprints. Premium $18/SF pricing on corners reflects proven demand for drive-through-capable pad sites.
  • Interior shift from retail to flex: 13 interior lots (30.1 acres) repositioned as flex/light industrial at $10/SF — realistic pricing for interior positions without road frontage. Eliminates speculative retail pricing on lots that brokers flagged as hard-to-place.
  • Multifamily anchors retained: Four large multifamily parcels (48.5 acres) at $7/SF preserved for EB-5/LIHTC compatibility and workforce housing demand. These parcels generate $14.8M in revenue while providing built-in consumer base for the retail clusters.
