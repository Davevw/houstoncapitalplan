MUD 584: The $23.4M Municipal Utility District bond remains the primary infrastructure financing mechanism, covering water, sewer, and drainage development. This is baked into all five design scenarios and repaid through district property taxes.

TIRZ (Tax Increment Reinvestment Zone): Discussions are underway to establish TIRZ designation for ITPH infrastructure that benefits the community outside the MUD boundary. The 24.27-acre detention/retention system and associated environmental infrastructure are potential qualifying elements. If approved, TIRZ could provide a second public-finance lever that stacks with the MUD bond, creating offsets for costs that extend beyond the district perimeter.

Strategic significance: If TIRZ stacks with the existing MUD bond, combined capital recovery could substantially offset the total development budget — fundamentally improving the project's risk-return profile for capital partners. Analysis is in progress.

Harvey Funds: An application seeking Harvey-related funds for retention pond and storm infrastructure costs is being researched for eligibility. These costs are directly tied to the detention system that serves both the development and the broader community watershed.

5.Retail Marketing & Reconfiguration

A retail specialist confirmed that several lots along the main corridor would be less enthusiastically received by the market than those at key intersections where project roads meet. Based on this feedback, the team is:

  • Clustering retail sites at high-traffic intersection corners where demand is strongest
  • Creating deeper pad-ready lots suitable for national tenants and quick-service restaurant operators
  • Reconfiguring interior corridor lots — converting less-marketable retail positions to flex, industrial, or multifamily use where demand exists
  • Consolidating the retail marketing plan into an optimized parcel strategy aligned with broker recommendations
Retail Strategy Shift

The reconfiguration concentrates retail value at the highest-traffic intersections while converting weaker retail positions into uses with stronger market demand. This approach maximizes per-acre revenue while reducing absorption risk on less-marketable retail parcels.

6.Design Concepts & Capital Plan Platform
